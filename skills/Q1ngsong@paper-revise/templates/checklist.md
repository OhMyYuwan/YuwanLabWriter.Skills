# Paper Revise Checklist

- [ ] Every reviewer comment has an answer or an explicit cross-reference.
- [ ] Each response starts politely and addresses the actual concern.
- [ ] The answer includes evidence, reasoning, or a concrete manuscript change.
- [ ] No response attacks reviewer competence or intent.
- [ ] Promised experiments, text edits, figures, or tables appear in the revised manuscript or are clearly marked as future/camera-ready when policy requires.
- [ ] Major objections receive more space than minor grammar issues.
- [ ] Novelty responses explain insight, not just component names.
- [ ] Factual-error corrections are polite and evidence-based.
- [ ] Scope disagreements cite venue/journal fit when possible.
- [ ] Line/section/table/figure references are specific.
- [ ] AC/AE message, if used, is short and factual.
