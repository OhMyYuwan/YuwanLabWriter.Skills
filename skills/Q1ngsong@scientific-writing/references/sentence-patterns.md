# Sentence Patterns

Use conventional phrasing to reduce ambiguity and reviewer friction, especially for non-native English writing.

## Introduction

- "X plays a critical role in..."
- "Despite extensive work on X, Y remains unclear."
- "A key limitation of prior work is..."
- "To address this gap, we..."
- "Our main contributions are..."

## Methods

- "We used X to measure Y."
- "Samples were prepared as follows."
- "The procedure involved three steps."
- "Data were analyzed using..."
- "Statistical significance was assessed by..."

## Results

- "Figure X shows that..."
- "Table X summarizes..."
- "Compared with the control group, ..."
- "No significant difference was observed between..."
- "These results indicate that..."

## Discussion

- "These findings suggest that..."
- "One possible explanation is..."
- "This result is consistent with..."
- "A limitation of this study is..."
- "Future work should examine..."

## Hedging

| Strength | Examples | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Strong | demonstrate, establish, show | When evidence is direct and strong. |
| Moderate | suggest, indicate, support | Default for most empirical interpretation. |
| Weak | may, might, could | For plausible but uncertain explanations. |

## Avoid

- "It should be emphasized that..."
- "It is well known that..."
- "very", "really", "quite"
- Long chains of "of"
- Ambiguous "this" without a noun
- "A study on..." as a title opener
